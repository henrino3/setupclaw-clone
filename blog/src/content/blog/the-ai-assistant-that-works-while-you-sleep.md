# The AI Assistant That Works While You Sleep

You've tried ChatGPT. Maybe you use AI writing tools for emails or summaries. They're useful for one-off tasks.

But what if your assistant kept working after you logged off?

## What "Always On" Means

A real AI assistant doesn't wait for you to open a tab and ask questions.

**Morning routine example:**
You wake up to find your agent has already:
- Flagged 3 urgent emails that need replies today
- Prepared briefing docs for your 10am meeting (with relevant context from past conversations)
- Scanned industry news and surfaced 2 articles relevant to your current project
- Checked project status across 4 tools and flagged one blocker

All of this happened at 6am. While you were asleep.

## The Shift

**Before agents:**
- You remember to check things
- You batch process emails when you have time
- Stuff falls through cracks when you're busy

**With an always-on agent:**
- Monitoring happens continuously
- Processing happens in the background
- You get alerts only when something needs your attention

It's less about speed and more about removing cognitive overhead.

## What Makes This Possible

ChatGPT closes when you close the tab. An always-on agent needs:

1. **Infrastructure** — Cloud-hosted, runs 24/7 (not on your laptop)
2. **Integrations** — Connected to your email, calendar, Slack, project tools
3. **Context** — Understands your priorities and how you work
4. **Boundaries** — Can act autonomously within limits you set

SetupClaw handles the technical setup so you can skip the DevOps learning curve.

## What This Looks Like in Practice

**Deal monitoring (finance/M&A):**
Agent scans deal databases overnight, pre-researches companies that match your criteria, and delivers a curated list each morning. The 2 hours you used to spend before 9am? Now automated.

**Client work (consulting):**
Agent drafts proposals from templates, populates them with client-specific data, and queues them for your review. You edit instead of starting from scratch.

**Hiring pipeline:**
Agent monitors new applicants, flags strong candidates, prepares interview packets with resume highlights and role fit analysis.

**Research:**
Agent scans new papers in your field, summarizes relevant ones, manages citations, and drafts literature review sections.

The pattern: high-value repetitive work that needs judgment but not genius.

## The "But I Can't Code" Thing

You don't need to.

You talk to your agent in plain English: "Every Monday morning, send me a digest of new M&A deals in healthcare tech."

The agent figures out the technical details. No Python, no APIs, no YAML files.

## What Autonomy Looks Like

Your agent won't send emails as you without approval (unless you explicitly configure that).

But it will:
- Draft emails for your review
- Update spreadsheets and tracking systems
- Create calendar events
- Flag items that need your attention
- Move tasks through project stages

You set the boundaries. It operates inside them.

## Reality Check

**This isn't magic.** Setting up an effective agent takes:
- 1-2 hours of initial configuration (us understanding your workflow)
- 2 weeks of tuning (adjusting autonomy levels and priorities)
- Ongoing feedback ("stop doing X, do more of Y")

And there are limits. Agents are good at:
- Monitoring and flagging
- Research and summarization
- Drafting and formatting
- Repetitive judgment calls

Agents aren't good at:
- High-stakes negotiations
- Creative strategy
- Building relationships
- Anything that requires deep human context

## The Test

How much of your day goes to work that a smart assistant could handle if they worked around the clock?

If it's more than 2 hours, you might benefit from an always-on agent.
