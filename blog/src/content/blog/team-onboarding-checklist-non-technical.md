# The Pre-Onboarding Checklist: Preparing Your Team for Their New AI Agent (March 2026)

At SetupClaw, we've seen it all. From the most efficient AI-driven teams to organizations that struggle with AI adoption. The difference? Preparation. Here's your non-technical guide to making your first agent onboarding a success.

### 1. Define the "Boundary of Trust"
An AI agent is only as useful as the tasks you trust it with. Before we arrive to set up your agent, define the "No-Go" zones. What decisions must *always* be made by a human? What sensitive data (e.g., employee performance reviews, financial passwords) should your agent never see? Having these boundaries defined saves hours during the configuration phase.

### 2. Identify the "Repeatable Pain"
Every team has a task that everyone hates. It's usually high-volume, low-creativity, and essential. This is where your agent should start. Whether it's sorting through support tickets, summarizing weekly project meetings, or cleaning up data in a CRM, identify the top three "repeatable pain points" that your team would happily hand off tomorrow.

### 3. Clean Your Data (A Little)
You don't need a perfectly organized database, but your agent will perform significantly better if its primary data sources (Google Drive, Slack, Notion) aren't full of outdated information. Encourage your team to spend 30 minutes deleting old drafts or archiving "Done" channels. Think of it like tidying up before the cleaners arrive.

### 4. Nominate an "Agent Champion"
Successful AI adoption needs a human point of contact. This isn't necessarily the person who knows the most about AI. It's the person who understands your team's workflow the best. They will be the one who "coaches" the agent in its first few weeks, giving it feedback and ensuring it's actually saving time, not adding work.

### 5. Set Expectations (The "Trainee" Mindset)
The biggest mistake teams make is thinking of an AI agent as a "finished" software product. It's not. It's more like a highly capable, tireless intern. It will make mistakes in the beginning. It will need to be told "not like that, like this." Preparing your team for this "coaching period" prevents early frustration and ensures long-term success.

### Ready to Start?
A few hours of prep can save weeks of trial and error. Your agent is ready to work — is your team ready to lead it?
